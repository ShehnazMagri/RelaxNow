Emotional Wellness Index

What drives you to act? The hedonistic principle says that our willingness to attain happiness (pleasure) and avoid pain, motivates us to act and work towards a goal. This is why we, as parents, partners and working professionals behave in a way that guarantees us happiness. But have you ever checked what your level of happiness is?

The following scale will help you understand your present happiness levels. The Goulston Vohra Scale of Happiness is a 7-item scale developed by Dr. Mark Goulston and Dr. Sandeep Vohra to assess how happy you are and help you understand how your happiness levels can be increased.

Here's what you need before taking the test:

  • • You will be asked to answer questions that will help you understand your level of happiness.
  • • You will have to answer all of them to complete the test successfully.
  • • Respond to each of the following questions on a scale of 0 to 10 with 0 meaning ‘Absolutely not’ ,5 meaning ‘Sometimes’ and 10 meaning ‘Yes, absolutely’.

This is like your mental temperature and the EWI is the thermometer. Once you take this intense test with honesty and as much clarity as you can muster, all the answers you need will be revealed through, wait for it, the Emotional Wellness Index (this multicolour bar, in case it wasn't obvious!)

What does it mean?
Green denotes no stress
Red is the other extreme denoting maximum stress requiring professional help
Blue/Yellow/Orange indicate varying degrees of mental stress
By answering 55 questions related to your life in whole, we can glean the level of stress in your life and, based on your answers, tell you what kind of help you need.
